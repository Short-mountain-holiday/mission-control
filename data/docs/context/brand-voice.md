# SMH Brand Voice Guide

> Every word written on behalf of Short Mountain Holiday should sound like it came from the same
> place: elevated, restorative, quietly confident. Not a generic rental. Not a luxury hotel.
> Something better — a retreat built with intention.

---

## The Voice in Three Words

**Intentional · Calming · Reliable**

---

## Tone Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| Warm but not casual | Overly familiar, bro-ey, or forced fun |
| Confident but not boastful | Overloaded superlatives ("amazing", "incredible") |
| Evocative — paint a picture | Just list features ("hot tub, fire pit, WiFi") |
| Let the setting do the work | Try too hard to sell it |
| Present tense, active voice | Passive or corporate language |
| Concise — one image is worth ten adjectives | Over-explaining or hedging |

## Words and Phrases We Use

- Escape, retreat, restore, unwind
- Thoughtful, intentional, curated
- Panoramic, private, secluded
- Elevated, refined
- Nature meets comfort
- Quiet, calm, still
- Crafted, designed, built with care
- A reset, a pause, a breath
- The mountain, the land, the views

---

## Words and Phrases We Never Use

| Banned | Why |
| --- | --- |
| **Cozy** | Too generic — every Airbnb says this |
| **Rustic** | Implies rough edges; we're not that |
| **Glamping** | Positions us wrong — sounds like a tent with a chandelier |
| **Unique** | Overused; show it, don't say it |
| **Amazing** | Filler superlative — find the specific thing that's amazing |
| **Perfect for...** | Cliché listing copy |
| **Nestled** | Fine to keep in old Airbnb descriptions but avoid in new copy |
| **Cozy** | (Repeated because it keeps sneaking in) |

## Voice by Channel

### Airbnb / VRBO Listings
- Lead with the feeling, not the feature inventory
- Use second person ("you", "your stay") to place the guest in the experience
- Keep paragraphs short — mobile-first
- No emoji in body copy

### Guest Messages (pre-arrival, during stay)
- Warm and personal — we're hosts, not a corporation
- Clear and scannable — guests read these quickly
- Anticipate the follow-up question in your answer
- Sign off as Micah or the SMH team, not just "the host"

### Social Media (Instagram)
- Shorter is better — one idea, one image, one caption
- Mood-first copy: set the scene before the detail
- Invite them in without being salesy
- Light use of single-line breaks for rhythm

### Review Responses
- Thank them specifically — use a detail from their review
- Never defensive, even when a review is critical
- Gracious, brief, and genuine

## Tone Examples

### Feature → Voice transformation

| Feature-only (avoid) | Voice-driven (use) |
| --- | --- |
| "Has a hot tub" | "Unwind in your private hot tub — the view from it alone is worth the trip." |
| "Fiber WiFi available" | "We hope you don't have to use the Fiber WiFi but, it's there if you must." |
| "2 bedrooms, sleeps 4" | "A king bed with panoramic views downstairs. Two beds in the loft for the kids or a second couple." |
| "No oven" | "Full kitchen with convection cooktop, griddle, and Nespresso — everything you need." |
| "30 acres" | "Thirty private acres. You might not see another soul the whole trip." |

## What "Elevated But Not Snobbish" Means in Practice

SMH is not a five-star hotel. We're not cold, formal, or distant.
But we're also not a $79/night Airbnb trying too hard to be fun.

The sweet spot: **luxury of feeling**, **simplicity of experience**.
Guests should feel like the place was made for them without feeling like they have to perform for it.

Write like you'd speak to a couple who just arrived after a long week. They're smart, tired,
and excited. They don't need the hard sell — they already booked. Help them exhale.
