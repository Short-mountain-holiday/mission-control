# Short Mountain Holiday (SMH) — Living Summary (from our chats)

> A comprehensive, "single source of truth" snapshot of what I've learned about Short Mountain Holiday from our conversations: who you are, what SMH is, what it's not, what you stand for, what's been hard, what you've learned, and what you're building toward.

---

## 1) What Short Mountain Holiday is

**Short Mountain Holiday (SMH)** is a **luxury micro-resort** you and Amber (wife) are building and operating in **Liberty, Tennessee** (about 1 hr 15 min from East Nashville). The property includes:
- **A cabin** Currently, our residence on the weekends while we build but, someday perhaps another rental.
- **Two geodesic domes** (notably: **North Ridge Dome** + **South Cove Dome**) with a third one **East Hill Dome** under construction.
- A growing set of on-site amenities and infrastructure you're actively designing, building, and refining. (Notably: a nordic hydro-spa including custom sauna, cold plunge, heated rest pool, large fire place feature, outdoor showers and small self-serve store.)

At its core, SMH is:
- A **crafted escape**: calm, nature-forward, intentional.
- A **hospitality experience** you treat like a product: high standards, repeatable systems, and constant iteration.
- A **hands-on founder build**: you personally design and install infrastructure, automation, and guest experience details.

---

## 2) What SMH is NOT

SMH is not:
- A generic "rental arbitrage / quick flip" Airbnb.
- A set-it-and-forget-it property with minimal attention to detail.
- A party property.
- A mass-scale resort or high-volume operation (at least not the identity you've expressed so far).
- A purely aesthetic project with weak systems — your approach is "beautiful *and* operationally sound."

---

## 3) Who you are (as hosts / builders)

### Identity
You and Amber come across as:
- **High-care hosts** who want guests to feel held, not "processed."
- **Builders** who don't just buy solutions — you architect them (DIY + automation + iteration).
- **Operators** who think in checklists, workflows, reliability, and guest outcomes.
- **Taste-driven**: you care about vibe, design, experience, and the "feeling" of the stay.

### Your operating posture
- **You're a Superhost** and treat that standard as part of the brand promise.
- You're willing to do tedious work to protect the guest experience.
- You want a system that can grow beyond you… but right now a lot still depends on you.
- We are happy to hire contractors on an as-needed basis including handymen and cleaners but on the whole, we do not wish to have employees.
- This is a lifestyle business. Meant to be our full time income (aiming for north of $250k/yr).

## 5) Key pain points (themes that keep showing up)

### A) Infrastructure reliability in a remote-ish context
- **WiFi coverage across multiple buildings** on ~4 acres has been a recurring challenge.
- Buildings are spread out, and connectivity needs to be dependable for guests.
- You've actively implemented **repeaters/extenders** and care about SSID clarity per structure.

### B) Utilities and "hidden systems"
You're constantly thinking about:
- Temperature limits for equipment modules
- Safe mounting locations (shed heat, exterior wall, under-deck, wall cavities)
- Electrical rough-in decisions, code compliance, and long-term serviceability

### C) Pests and nature intrusions
- The **white Pacific Domes geodesic dome** was **taken over by Asian beetles (ladybugs)**.
- You deal with "beautiful outdoors" realities: bugs, seasonal issues, algae in pond, etc.

### D) Guest experience quality control (at scale)
- As reservations increase, you want consistent excellence without having to "white-glove" every stay manually.
- You're building toward automation to reduce mistakes and improve consistency.
- Want to automate it on our side but have it feel bespoke and tailored to our guests.

### E) Systems fragmentation (tools, workflows, messaging)
- You use **Hostaway** as your PMS and you're building automation around it.
- You want messaging, check-ins, FAQs, and operational tasks to happen reliably even when you're busy.
- Home Assistant has been initially set up but virtually unused. Would prefer this be our central hub for everything "smart" on the property.

### F) Emotional/mental load
Even when you're excited, there's a consistent thread of:
- "A lot depends on me."
- The weight of being builder + operator + host.
- The tension between meaningful projects and fatigue.

## 6) Things you've learned (hard-earned insights)

### A) Every "comfort feature" is actually a system
A hot tub, sauna, wifi, water, heat, lighting — each one:
- Has failure modes
- Requires maintenance rhythms
- Needs clear guest instructions
- Needs a fallback plan

### B) Clarity prevents support calls
Your direction tends to move toward:
- Simple instructions
- Repeatable checklists
- Automation that nudges guests at the right moment

### C) Reliability beats novelty
You like impressive ideas, but you consistently pull decisions back to:
- "Will it work every time?"
- "Can I service it easily?"
- "What happens when something fails at 10pm?"

### D) "Design" is also operational design
You think like a product person:
- Guest flow
- Wayfinding (including WiFi SSIDs per structure)
- Reducing confusion and friction
- Ensuring a consistent "feel"

## 7) Struggles you have (stated + implied)

### Operational struggles
- Coordinating many parallel projects: electrical, networking, pests, furnishing, messaging, automation.
- Balancing "do it right" with time constraints and energy constraints.
- Keeping the build moving while also being in active hosting mode.

### Human struggles
- The constant mental context switching: family life, your other work, and SMH operations.
- The burden of being the person who holds the whole plan in your head.
