## Seasonal Considerations for Guests

### Spring (March - May)
- Peak season ramp-up begins mid-March — booking intent climbs through April
- Wildflowers and green-up across the property — strong visual content season
- Cool mornings / mild afternoons — hot tub especially appealing
- Spring equinox March 20 — Campaign 3 "Sky" first post
- Tennessee dogwood and redbud bloom: late March through April
- Easter weekend: family travel spike
- Spring break windows vary by school district — Nashville-area breaks typically mid-March

### Summer (June - August)
- Peak season — highest occupancy potential, longest days
- Hot tub use shifts to evening/night (daytime heat)
- Fireflies on the property — June is peak firefly season in Middle Tennessee
- Center Hill Lake (30 min away) — swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding
- July 4th weekend: premium pricing window, 3-night minimum typical
- Outdoor dining and fire pit are major draws
- Summer solstice (June 20-21) — Campaign 3 "Sky" content opportunity

### Fall
- **Layer up** — temperatures drop quickly at elevation; guests should bring extra layers
- Fall foliage is a draw — typically peaks mid-October in Cannon County
